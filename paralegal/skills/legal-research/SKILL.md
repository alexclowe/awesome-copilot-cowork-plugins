## Core competencies

**Case analysis:**
- Identify the holding, reasoning, and dicta in judicial opinions
- Distinguish cases on their facts -- explain why a precedent does or does not apply
- Trace the development of legal doctrines through lines of cases
- Identify the weight of authority -- binding vs. persuasive, majority vs. minority rules
- Recognize when cases have been overruled, distinguished, or limited by subsequent decisions
- Synthesize multiple authorities into a coherent statement of the law

**Statutory interpretation:**
- Apply canons of construction: plain meaning, legislative intent, ejusdem generis, noscitur a sociis, expressio unius
- Read statutes in context of the entire statutory scheme
- Identify applicable regulations and agency interpretations
- Track amendments and effective dates
- Distinguish mandatory ("shall") from permissive ("may") language
- Identify definitions sections and cross-references

**Regulatory research:**
- Navigate federal and state regulatory frameworks
- Identify applicable administrative rules and agency guidance
- Understand the hierarchy: statute > regulation > agency guidance > informal interpretation
- Track proposed rulemaking and comment periods
- Identify enforcement patterns and compliance requirements

**Precedent identification:**
- Formulate effective search strategies using key terms, legal concepts, and fact patterns
- Identify controlling authority for the specific jurisdiction and court level
- Find analogous cases from other jurisdictions when local authority is sparse
- Locate secondary sources for background and emerging issues
- Check that authorities remain good law (noting that AI cannot perform real-time citator checks)

## IRAC methodology

Apply the IRAC framework systematically:

**Issue:**
- Frame issues precisely -- include the legal standard, the key disputed facts, and the specific question
- Break complex questions into discrete sub-issues
- Order issues logically -- threshold issues first, then dependent issues

**Rule:**
- State the governing legal standard clearly and completely
- Include the elements or factors of the applicable test
- Note the burden of proof and standard of review
- Cite controlling authority for each rule statement
- Identify any exceptions or qualifications

**Application:**
- Apply each element or factor to the specific facts
- Draw explicit comparisons to precedent cases
- Address both favorable and unfavorable facts
- Acknowledge gaps in the factual record
- Consider alternative interpretations

**Conclusion:**
- State the likely outcome clearly
- Qualify with confidence level (likely, probable, uncertain, depends on)
- Identify what additional facts or legal developments could change the analysis
- Provide practical recommendations

## Citation formats

**Bluebook (general federal/state):**
- Cases: *Party v. Party*, Volume Reporter Page (Court Year)
- Statutes: Title U.S.C. section (Year)
- Regulations: Volume C.F.R. section (Year)
- Use pinpoint citations to specific pages or sections

**Note on AI-generated citations:**
- All citations generated by AI must be independently verified
- Case names, reporter volumes, page numbers, and holdings may be inaccurate
- Always confirm that cited cases remain good law
- Verify statutory citations against current codifications

## Research organization

- Maintain a research trail -- document search terms, databases, and results
- Organize findings by issue, not by source
- Create case charts comparing key cases on relevant factors
- Flag gaps in research and areas needing further investigation
- Note the date research was conducted -- law changes
